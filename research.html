Our Research -- To conquer aging, theory must go first!

To study the method of life extension, we must initially identify the cause and biochemical mechanism of aging, and subsequently design the solutions based on this cause and mechanism. Given that the issue of aging is highly complex, if we disregard the causes and mechanisms of aging and merely rely on chance in the laboratory, it is tantamount to shooting at targets blindfolded, with nearly no possibility of success. Hence, it can be stated that money, laboratories, and technicians alone cannot achieve rejuvenation; we must have a theory. Therefore, we focus on theory.

There are over 300 theories regarding aging, and almost every theory has given rise to an anti-aging method. However, the majority of these theories are incorrect. Consequently, as of now, no method worldwide has succeeded in rejuvenating humans or significantly extending the human lifespan. Our task is to eliminate erroneous theories and methods through logical reasoning, thereby enabling us to avoid detours and reduce unnecessary waste, because, after all, our time and funds are limited and cannot be expended without constraint.

For advanced problems, theoretical ability holds greater significance than practical ability, as theory can provide the correct answer and guide the research direction and methodology. For instance, although Albert Einstein was unskilled with his hands and even made crooked small benches, he was capable of theorizing that gravity has fluctuations, and scientists were able to find evidence of gravitational waves based on his theory.

Current Projects

Aging theory

Given that every organism has a relatively fixed schedule of growth and development, mature, aging, and death, the essence of aging is a genetic program rather than some passive or accidental events occurring in the cell, such as the accumulation of harmful waste, mitochondrial and DNA mutations and accumulations, etc. Moreover, the program at the individual level is regulated by the program at the DNA level.

Through rigorous logical reasoning and experimental research, we found that the root cause of cellular senescence and Hayflick limits are telomere DNA and rDNA. In other word, the aging of tissue, organ, individual is caused by the aging of adult stem cells and the aging of adult stem cells is caused by the reduction of telomere DNA and rDNA copy numbers. Consequently, to surpass the limitations of lifespan, it is essential to extend telomeres and rDNA. Moreover, merely reversing epigenetic inheritance or modulating signaling pathways will prove ineffective.

Causality of aging hallmarks

Aging causes constitutes the fundamental factor guiding the aging of the body, and the aging phenomenon / aging hallmarks / aging results pertains to the various manifestations exhibited by the body after aging. There may be only one cause of aging, but there may be thousands of aging phenomena. For instance, the aging of facial skin leads to the formation of wrinkles and hair turns white. Lipofuscin is deposited in the inner cytoplasm, protein renewal rate decreases, cross-linked denateration protein increases, mitochondrial DNA mutation accumulation, protein glycosylation and cross-linking and other aging phenomena are the aging results, which is reversible.

In our exploration of aging theories from a causal perspective, we have observed that many aging-related changes have been observed and touted as drivers of aging. To address the issue of aging, it is imperative to identify its root causes. This is because targeting the results of aging without addressing the root causes may result in limited efficacy, as interventions focused solely on results fail to address the fundamental issues. By targeting the root causes, both the results and the root causes can be addressed, leading to significant extensions in lifespan.

For example, in 1973, Tappel found that adult mice fed with a diet containing vitamin E for one year indeed had less lipofuscin, but no reduction in mortality (Shikai Xu, Ed., Pharmacology of Anti-Aging, p. 256). Conclusion: Lipofuscin deposition is an aging phenomenon, not a cause of aging. There are more than 300 theories to explain why people age, but the vast majority of them mistakenly treat aging results as cause.

If the aging phenomenon is utilized as a basis for developing solutions aimed at extending life or achieving rejuvenation, then this initial approach is fundamentally wrong. It represents a bottomless pit; regardless of the time and financial resources invested, the ultimate outcome will be failure.

Aging and degenerative diseases

Leonard Hayflick once argued that, Richard Adelman published a paper titled "The Study of Aging in Alzheimer's Disease"in 1995, in which he argued that the NIA had disproportionately invested heavily in Alzheimer's research, leaving almost no funding available for research that cared about the basic principles of aging. It's reasonable to research age-related diseases, but it's absurd to ignore the biggest risk factors for them. We need to understand that even if we completely cured or even eliminated all the diseases of aging, humans would still age.

Most people pay more attention to those age-related diseases because they only see their family or friends suffering from these diseases, but they never realize that the most important part in the name of "age-related disease" is "aging", not "disease".

Alzheimer's disease, liver cirrhosis, pulmonary fibrosis, cancer, type 2 diabetes, and obesity in the elderly are all caused by aging, and there is no medicine that can provide a radical cure. Consequently, addressing the root cause of aging is more effective than tackling each disease individually.
